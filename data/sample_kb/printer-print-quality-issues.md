# SOP: Print Quality Issues (Streaks, Faded, Smudged)

## Issue
Printer produces output with streaks, faded text/images, smudging, or incorrect colors — the printer is otherwise functional (accepts jobs, doesn't show offline), this is purely an output quality issue.

## Common Causes
1. Low or empty toner/ink cartridge
2. Print heads or drum unit dirty/needs cleaning (laser printers) or clogged nozzles (inkjet)
3. Incorrect paper type setting causing improper toner/ink adhesion
4. Aging drum unit or fuser needing replacement (laser printers, high page-count devices)

## Resolution Steps
1. Check toner/ink levels via the printer's control panel or web interface first — this resolves the majority of quality complaints.
2. Run the built-in cleaning cycle from the printer's maintenance menu (available on virtually all business printers) — for streaking or faded output this is the standard first fix.
3. Confirm the paper type setting matches what's loaded (e.g. plain vs. glossy) — a mismatch causes poor adhesion and quality issues that look like a hardware fault but aren't.
4. If cleaning doesn't resolve streaking on a laser printer, check the drum unit's page count against its rated lifespan — drums degrade gradually and need replacement, not just cleaning, past their rated cycle count.
5. For color inaccuracy specifically, confirm color calibration hasn't drifted — most business printers have a calibration routine in the maintenance menu.

## Escalation
If toner/drum replacement doesn't resolve the issue, escalate to Facilities/vendor support for a hardware service call — repeated consumable replacement without improvement suggests a mechanical fault.

## SLA
P4 (single device, cosmetic quality issue) — target resolution 1 business day.
