# SOP: Role Change / Access Update Request

## Issue
An employee has changed roles, teams, or departments internally, and their system access needs to be updated to match — both adding new access and removing old access that's no longer appropriate.

## Resolution Steps
1. Confirm the role change with HR or the new manager, including the effective date — access changes should align with the actual transition date, not be done early or late.
2. Compare the employee's current group memberships and app access against the standard access template for their new role.
3. Add access matching the new role's template first, so there's no gap in the employee's ability to work.
4. Remove access tied specifically to the old role (old department shared drives, old distribution lists, old team's Teams channels) — this step is the one most often skipped, and it's a real security/least-privilege concern, not just tidiness.
5. Confirm with both the old and new manager if there's any shared/transitional access needed during a handover period, and set an expiration date for that transitional access rather than leaving it open-ended.

## Common Follow-up Issues
- Employee reports losing access to something they still need — verify against the role template before re-granting; it may be legitimately outside their new role's scope and need manager justification.

## Escalation
Role changes involving access to sensitive systems (Finance, HR, Legal, production systems) require the new manager's and the relevant data owner's approval before granting — don't provision on the role-change notice alone.

## SLA
P4 (standard access update) — target resolution 2 business days.
