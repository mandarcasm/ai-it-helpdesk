# SOP: Equipment Return & Asset Tracking

## Issue
An employee is leaving, changing roles, or has broken/excess equipment that needs to be returned and logged back into asset inventory.

## Resolution Steps
1. Look up the employee's assigned assets in the asset management system (laptop serial number, monitor, dock, peripherals) before requesting return — don't rely on the employee's memory of what they have.
2. For departing employees, coordinate return timing with HR's offboarding checklist — equipment return and account deprovisioning should happen close together, not weeks apart.
3. On physical return, verify the asset tag/serial number matches the inventory record before marking it returned.
4. Inspect condition and note any damage in the asset record — this matters for warranty claims and loss/damage policy.
5. Wipe the device (factory reset or Autopilot reset) before it re-enters the deployment pool — never re-issue a device to someone else without a full wipe.
6. Update the asset management system status to "Available" (redeployable) or "Retired" (end of life) as appropriate.

## Common Follow-up Issues
- Remote employees returning equipment by mail — provide a prepaid shipping label and confirm delivery before closing the ticket, don't assume it arrived.
- Employee claims equipment was already returned but it's not showing in inventory — check with reception/mailroom logs before escalating as a loss.

## Escalation
Equipment reported lost or stolen (not simply "not yet returned") must be escalated to Security immediately, since this may involve data on the device — see SOP "Lost or Stolen Device Reported."

## SLA
P4 (standard return, role change or planned departure) — target resolution 5 business days.
